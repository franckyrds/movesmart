🏨 Where to Stay in Marseille?

⚓ Old Port

The heart of Marseille. With lively waterfront cafés, departures to the Calanques, seafood restaurants and historic quays, it's the perfect base for exploring the city on foot.

🎨 Le Panier

Marseille's oldest neighborhood charms visitors with its colorful streets, picturesque squares, artists' studios and authentic Mediterranean atmosphere. Ideal for a memorable and character-filled stay.

🌊 Prado

Just steps from the beaches, Parc Borély and the Corniche Kennedy, Prado is the perfect area to enjoy the Mediterranean, relax by the sea and try a variety of water sports.

🏛️ Joliette & Euroméditerranée

A modern district combining contemporary architecture, shopping, the Mucem, La Major Cathedral and Les Terrasses du Port. An excellent choice for a comfortable city break.

⚽ Castellane

Well connected by metro, Castellane offers quick access to the Old Port, the beaches and the Orange Vélodrome. A lively neighborhood known for its excellent value for money.

🌅 Corniche Kennedy

For a stay by the sea, it's hard to beat the Corniche. Enjoy hotels overlooking the Mediterranean, spectacular sunsets and easy access to Marseille's beaches.

From the historic charm of Le Panier to seaside hotels along the Corniche, the lively atmosphere of the Old Port and the beaches of Prado, every neighborhood offers a different way to experience Marseille, whatever your budget or travel style.

✈️ Before You Visit Marseille

🗣️ Language

French is the official language. In the main tourist areas, many people working in hotels, restaurants and attractions also speak English.

💶 Currency

France uses the euro (€). Credit and debit cards are accepted almost everywhere, although some markets and small local shops still prefer cash.

🔌 Power Plugs

France uses Type C and Type E power outlets with a 230 V supply. Most European travelers won't need a power adapter.

📅 Ideal Length of Stay

Three to four days is ideal for exploring the Old Port, the Calanques, Notre-Dame de la Garde, Marseille's main neighborhoods and enjoying a day trip nearby.

☀️ Best Time to Visit

April to June and September to October offer pleasant temperatures and fewer crowds. Summer brings a lively atmosphere but is also the busiest season.

💰 Average Budget

Plan on spending around €100 to €220 per day depending on your accommodation, restaurants and chosen activities. Boat excursions can increase your daily budget.

⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid in Marseille

☀️ Underestimating the Sun

The Mediterranean sun can be extremely strong, especially during summer. Bring plenty of water, a hat and sunscreen, particularly when hiking in the Calanques.

🥾 Hiking in the Calanques Without Preparation

Some hiking trails are demanding, and access may be restricted because of wildfire risks. Always check the latest access conditions before setting out.

🚗 Driving Everywhere

Traffic can be heavy in the city center, and parking is often difficult. Public transport, walking and shuttle boats are usually much more convenient.

👜 Forgetting About Your Belongings

As in any major tourist city, keep an eye on your personal belongings, especially around the Old Port, on public transport and in busy markets.

🌊 Only Visiting the Old Port

Marseille truly shines when you explore its diverse neighborhoods, the Calanques, the Frioul Islands, Notre-Dame de la Garde and nearby gems such as Cassis and Aix-en-Provence.

❓ Marseille FAQ

How many days do you need to visit Marseille?

Three to four days are enough to explore Marseille's main attractions, the Calanques National Park and enjoy a day trip to Cassis or Aix-en-Provence.

When is the best time to visit Marseille?

Spring and early autumn offer the best weather for enjoying the city, the Mediterranean coastline and hiking while avoiding the intense summer heat.

Can you visit Marseille without a car?

Yes. The metro, trams, buses, ferry boats and walking make it easy to explore Marseille's main neighborhoods and attractions without driving.

Are the Calanques open all year round?

Yes, but some hiking trails may be temporarily closed during summer because of wildfire risks. Always check access conditions before your visit.

Which area is best for a first stay?

The Old Port (Vieux-Port) is the best base for first-time visitors thanks to its central location, restaurants, public transport and proximity to the city's main landmarks.

Is Marseille an expensive destination?

Marseille is generally more affordable than Paris or the French Riviera, with accommodation and restaurants available for every budget.
