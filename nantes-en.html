🇫🇷 Nantes at a glance

Located on the banks of the Loire, Nantes is a vibrant city combining historic heritage, contemporary creativity and an excellent quality of life. Once the capital of the Dukes of Brittany, it is as appealing for its rich history as it is for its innovative spirit.

From the historic centre to the Île de Nantes district, from the Machines of the Isle to the banks of the Erdre, every neighbourhood reveals a different atmosphere shaped by culture, nature, gastronomy and urban art.

With cultural events, waterfront walks, original experiences and remarkable heritage, Nantes has become one of the most attractive cities in western France.

✈️ Before visiting Nantes

🗣️ Language

French is the official language. English is commonly spoken at the main tourist attractions, hotels and many restaurants.

💶 Currency

The euro (€) is used throughout Nantes. Credit and debit cards are widely accepted in shops, restaurants and on public transport.

🔌 Power plugs

France uses Type C and Type E electrical sockets with a 230 V supply. Most European travellers will not need an adapter.

📅 Ideal length of stay

Two to three days are enough to discover the city's main districts, the Machines of the Isle, the Château des Ducs de Bretagne and Nantes' iconic attractions.

🌤️ Best time to visit

From May to September, Nantes usually enjoys its most pleasant weather for outdoor walks, terraces and cultural events.

💰 Average budget

Plan on spending around €90 to €180 per day for a comfortable stay, depending on your accommodation, activities and restaurant choices.

⚠️ Mistakes to avoid in Nantes

🐘 Skipping the Machines of the Isle

This is Nantes' most iconic attraction. Even during a short stay, it deserves a place on your itinerary.

🚶 Staying only in the city centre

Trentemoult, the banks of the Erdre, the Île de Nantes and the Beaujoire Floral Park reveal very different sides of the city.

🎟️ Booking major events too late

Hellfest, concerts, performances and popular experiences often sell out several weeks or even months in advance.

🚋 Overlooking public transport

The Naolib network provides easy access to most neighbourhoods and attractions and is often more convenient than driving through the city centre.

🌧️ Forgetting Nantes' weather

The oceanic climate can be unpredictable. Even in summer, packing a lightweight waterproof jacket is a good idea.

📅 Visiting Nantes in just one day

Between the historic centre, the Machines of the Isle, Trentemoult, museums and riverside walks, two or three days allow you to enjoy the city properly.

❓ Nantes FAQ

How many days do you need to visit Nantes?

Two to three days are ideal for discovering the city's main districts, the Machines of the Isle, the Château des Ducs de Bretagne, Trentemoult and Nantes' iconic attractions.

What is the must-do experience in Nantes?

The Machines of the Isle are Nantes' signature attraction. The Giant Elephant and the Marine Worlds Carousel attract visitors from around the world every year.

When is the best time to visit Nantes?

From May to September, temperatures are generally pleasant and the city hosts many events, including Le Voyage à Nantes and numerous summer festivals.

Is Nantes an expensive destination?

Nantes is generally more affordable than Paris and many other major European cities, offering excellent value for accommodation, restaurants and transport.

How do you get around Nantes?

The Naolib network makes it easy to travel around the city with trams, buses, self-service bicycles and other urban mobility solutions.

Which neighbourhoods should I explore first?

The historic centre, Graslin district, Île de Nantes, Trentemoult and the banks of the Erdre are among the city's most interesting areas.

What can you do in Nantes with children?

The Machines of the Isle, the Jardin des Plantes, riverside walks along the Erdre and several Loire cruises are excellent family-friendly activities.

Is Nantes a good city for cycling?

Yes. Nantes has an extensive network of cycle paths and is regularly ranked among France's most bike-friendly cities.

Nantes is full of surprises

With the Loire, the Machines of the Isle, historic neighbourhoods, green spaces and its creative energy, Nantes offers a unique experience in western France.
