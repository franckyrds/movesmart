🏨 Where to Stay in Amsterdam

🌷 Jordaan

Amsterdam's most picturesque neighborhood. Peaceful canals, historic houses, art galleries, cozy cafés and independent boutiques create an authentic atmosphere that's perfect for a first visit.

🏛️ Centrum

The historic heart of Amsterdam, just steps from Dam Square, Central Station and the city's iconic canals. Ideal for exploring the main attractions on foot while enjoying the lively city center.

🎨 De Pijp

A vibrant and cosmopolitan district known for its trendy cafés, international restaurants and the famous Albert Cuyp Market. A great choice for discovering Amsterdam's creative and local side.

🌳 Oud-Zuid

Elegant and residential, Oud-Zuid is home to the Rijksmuseum, the Van Gogh Museum and Vondelpark. A peaceful and refined area ideal for travelers seeking comfort, culture and green spaces.

🎭 Leidseplein

The perfect district for nightlife. Packed with lively bars, concert venues, restaurants and theaters, Leidseplein is ideal for travelers who want to experience Amsterdam after dark.

🚢 Amsterdam Noord

Easily reached by free ferry from Central Station, Amsterdam Noord stands out for its contemporary architecture, cultural venues, revitalized industrial spaces and stunning city views. A fantastic choice for discovering Amsterdam's modern and creative side.

From charming canal-side boutique hotels in Jordaan to elegant stays in Oud-Zuid, lively accommodations in the city center and stylish design hotels in Amsterdam Noord, every traveler can find the perfect neighborhood to match their travel style.

✈️ Before You Go to Amsterdam

🗣️ Language

Dutch is the official language, but the vast majority of locals also speak excellent English.

💶 Currency

The Netherlands uses the euro (€). Card payments are accepted almost everywhere, even for small amounts.

🚲 Getting Around

Cycling is king in Amsterdam. Be careful around bike lanes, as they are often heavily used by locals.

📅 Ideal Length of Stay

Two to four days are enough to discover the main neighborhoods, must-see museums, historic canals and several of the city's signature experiences.

🌷 Best Time to Visit

Spring, especially between April and May, is particularly popular thanks to the tulip season and pleasant temperatures.

💰 Average Budget

Amsterdam is one of Northern Europe's most expensive capitals. A comfortable budget usually ranges from €150 to €300 per day, depending on your travel style.

⚠️ Mistakes to Avoid in Amsterdam

🚲 Underestimating the Bikes

Bike lanes are everywhere and heavily used. Crossing without looking or walking on a bike lane can quickly create dangerous situations.

🎟️ Booking Museums Too Late

The Van Gogh Museum, Anne Frank House and several popular experiences often sell out days, or even weeks, in advance.

🏨 Staying Too Far Away

Amsterdam is easy to explore on foot or by bike. Staying too far from the center can reduce the time you spend enjoying the sights and the city's unique atmosphere.

🌷 Only Visiting the Historic Center

Many travelers only explore the city center, while neighborhoods such as De Pijp, Jordaan and Amsterdam Noord are among the most enjoyable areas to discover.

🍺 Confusing Coffee Shops and Cafés

In the Netherlands, a coffee shop is a licensed establishment that sells cannabis. For coffee or pastries, head instead to a traditional café or a Brown Café.

❓ Amsterdam FAQ

How many days do you need to visit Amsterdam?

Two to four days are enough to explore the city's main neighborhoods, world-class museums, historic canals and unique atmosphere.

Which neighborhood is best for a first visit?

Jordaan is often considered the best area for a first stay thanks to its picturesque canals, charming cafés and central location.

Is Amsterdam an expensive destination?

Yes. Amsterdam is one of the most expensive cities in the Netherlands. Accommodation is usually the biggest expense, especially on weekends and during peak season.

When is the best time to visit Amsterdam?

Spring, especially from April to May, is a favorite thanks to blooming tulips and pleasant temperatures. Autumn is also a wonderful time to enjoy the city's peaceful canals.

Should I book attractions in advance?

Yes. The Van Gogh Museum, Anne Frank House, many canal cruises and other popular attractions often sell out several days or even weeks in advance.

Can you visit Amsterdam without a car?

Absolutely. Amsterdam is easy to explore on foot, by bike or using public transport. A car is generally unnecessary and can even be inconvenient in the city center.

Amsterdam Slows Down Time

Between the reflections on the canals, the endless flow of bicycles, the cafés along the waterfront and the historic façades around every corner, Amsterdam has the rare ability to make time slow down.
