🏨 Where to Stay in Palma de Mallorca

🏛️ Old Town

The perfect choice for a first visit to Palma. Wander between the cathedral, cobbled streets, traditional Mallorcan courtyards, boutique shops and excellent restaurants, all within walking distance in a charming Mediterranean setting.

🌴 Santa Catalina

Palma's trendiest neighbourhood, famous for its lively food market, tapas bars, creative restaurants and vibrant nightlife. Ideal for travellers looking to experience the city's local and contemporary atmosphere.

🌊 Portixol & Molinar

Once a traditional fishing district, this is now one of Palma's most pleasant areas. Seafront promenades, small beaches, waterfront cafés and a relaxed atmosphere make it a perfect choice for a romantic or peaceful stay.

🏖️ Playa de Palma

Perfect for a beach holiday with a long sandy shoreline, hotels with pools, restaurants, bars and plenty of water sports. Close to the airport, it's an excellent option for travellers seeking relaxation by the sea.

From the historic charm of Palma's Old Town to the lively atmosphere of Santa Catalina, the seaside promenade of Portixol and the beachfront resorts of Playa de Palma, every neighbourhood offers a different way to experience Mallorca.

✈️ Before You Travel to Mallorca

🇪🇸 Language

Spanish and Catalan (Mallorquín) are the island's official languages. English is widely spoken in tourist areas, while French is occasionally understood.

💶 Currency

The euro (€) is used throughout the island. Credit and debit cards are accepted almost everywhere, including shops, restaurants and tourist activities.

🚗 Getting Around

Renting a car is the best way to explore Mallorca's hidden coves, mountain villages and scenic viewpoints. If you're staying mainly in Palma, public transport is more than sufficient.

📅 Ideal Length of Stay

Four to seven days is enough to discover Palma, the villages of the Serra de Tramuntana, several stunning coves and enjoy some of the island's best sea activities.

☀️ Best Time to Visit

From April to June and from September to October, Mallorca enjoys pleasant temperatures, warm seas and fewer crowds than during the peak summer season.

💰 Average Budget

Plan on around €120 to €250 per person per day depending on the season, accommodation and activities. Booking several weeks in advance often secures the best prices.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid in Mallorca

🚗 Waiting Too Long to Rent a Car

Rental prices rise quickly during school holidays, and the most popular vehicles sell out fast. Booking early usually means lower prices and a wider choice.

🏖️ Arriving Too Late at the Coves

Beautiful beaches such as Caló des Moro, Cala Deià and Cala Varques become crowded very early in summer. Arriving in the morning offers a much more peaceful experience.

☀️ Underestimating the Mediterranean Sun

From May to September, temperatures can rise quickly. Bring plenty of water, sunscreen and a hat, especially if you're hiking or joining a boat excursion.

🥾 Underestimating Driving Times

Although Mallorca looks small on a map, the mountain roads are winding and slower than expected. Journeys through the Serra de Tramuntana often take longer than visitors anticipate.

🌊 Only Visiting Palma's Beaches

Palma has beautiful beaches, but Mallorca's most spectacular scenery is found in its hidden coves, mountain villages and breathtaking viewpoints in the north and west of the island.

❓ Mallorca FAQ

How many days do you need to visit Mallorca?

Four to seven days is ideal to explore Palma, the charming villages of the Serra de Tramuntana, several iconic coves and some of the island's top excursions.

When is the best time to visit Mallorca?

April to June and September to October offer the perfect balance of pleasant weather, warm sea temperatures and fewer crowds than the peak summer months.

Should you rent a car in Mallorca?

Yes, if you want to explore picturesque villages, secluded beaches and scenic coastal roads. If you're staying mainly in Palma, public transport is perfectly adequate.

What are the most beautiful beaches in Mallorca?

Caló des Moro, Cala Varques, Cala Deià, Es Trenc, Cala Mondragó and Cala Agulla are among the island's most beautiful beaches and coves.

Where should you stay on your first visit?

Palma is an excellent base for discovering the island. For a quieter experience, the charming towns of Sóller, Port de Sóller, Alcúdia and Pollença are also excellent choices.

Is Mallorca a good destination for families?

Absolutely. With safe beaches, boat trips, picturesque villages, aquariums, water parks and plenty of outdoor activities, Mallorca is a fantastic destination for a family holiday.
