🏨 Where to stay in Marrakech?

🕌 Medina

The historic heart of Marrakech. With traditional riads, lively souks, Jemaa el-Fna Square and the city's main landmarks, it is the ideal area to experience the authentic atmosphere of the Red City.

🌴 Gueliz

The modern side of Marrakech. Boutiques, cafés, restaurants, art galleries and contemporary hotels make Gueliz an excellent choice for a comfortable stay just minutes from the medina.

✨ Hivernage

Marrakech's elegant district. Luxury hotels, rooftop bars, spas, fine restaurants and vibrant nightlife make Hivernage a favourite among travellers looking for a stylish stay.

🌺 Palmeraie

Away from the city's hustle and bustle, the Palmeraie is home to many resorts with swimming pools, gardens and spas. A peaceful setting that remains close to Marrakech.

🏡 Kasbah

Located south of the medina, this quieter district offers easy access to the city walls, the Saadian Tombs and several historic palaces while preserving an authentic local atmosphere.

🌿 Agdal

Close to Marrakech's famous gardens and several luxury hotels, Agdal offers a spacious, modern and residential environment appreciated for its tranquillity and quick access to the city centre.

From charming riads hidden within the medina to luxury hotels in Hivernage, peaceful resorts in the Palmeraie and modern accommodation in Gueliz, Marrakech offers places to stay for every style of traveller.

✈️ Before you travel to Marrakech

🗣️ Language

Moroccan Arabic, known as Darija, and Berber are the most widely spoken languages. French is commonly understood in hotels, restaurants and shops, while English is increasingly spoken in tourist areas.

💳 Currency & payments

The local currency is the Moroccan dirham (MAD). Bank cards are accepted in many establishments, but it is advisable to carry some cash for souks, taxis, small shops and tips.

🚕 Getting around

The main sights of the medina are easy to explore on foot. For longer journeys, use official petit taxis or pre-booked transfers to avoid unnecessary negotiations.

📅 Ideal length of stay

Three to four days are enough to visit the main monuments, enjoy the gardens, explore the souks and spend a day on an excursion to the Atlas Mountains, Essaouira or the Ouzoud Waterfalls.

☀️ Best time to visit

Spring and autumn offer particularly pleasant temperatures. Summer daytime temperatures can exceed 40°C, while winter evenings are often much cooler.

🕌 Local customs

Morocco is a welcoming country where respectful clothing is appreciated, especially in religious places and traditional neighbourhoods. Asking permission before photographing local people is also an important sign of respect.

⚠️ Mistakes to avoid in Marrakech

🛍️ Buying without comparing prices

Bargaining is part of the local culture in the souks. Take the time to compare several shops before buying and keep the exchange friendly and good-natured.

🚕 Taking a taxi without agreeing on the fare

When the meter is not being used, ask for the fare before setting off. Ride-hailing services or pre-booked transfers can also help you avoid unpleasant surprises.

🥤 Forgetting to stay hydrated

The climate can be very hot for much of the year. Always carry a bottle of water, especially while exploring the medina or taking part in desert excursions.

📸 Taking photos without asking

Some people, particularly performers in Jemaa el-Fna Square or traders in the souks, may expect payment for photographs. Always ask for permission first.

☀️ Underestimating the sun

Even in spring, the Moroccan sun can be intense. A hat, sunglasses, sunscreen and regular breaks in the shade will make sightseeing much more comfortable.

🗺️ Staying only in the medina

Marrakech offers much more than its historic centre. Its gardens, the Palmeraie, Gueliz and the many excursions into the Atlas Mountains or towards the coast reveal the full diversity of the region.

❓ Marrakech FAQ

How many days do you need to visit Marrakech?

Three to four days are enough to discover the main monuments, gardens, souks and palaces while also allowing time for an excursion beyond the city.

Which neighbourhood is best for a first stay?

The medina is ideal for experiencing the traditional atmosphere of Marrakech, while Gueliz and Hivernage offer greater comfort, modern restaurants and a livelier nightlife scene.

Can you visit Marrakech without a car?

Yes. Most tourist attractions within the medina are easy to reach on foot. Taxis and organised excursions make it simple to explore the other neighbourhoods and surrounding areas.

What is the best time to visit?

March to May and September to November generally offer the best conditions for exploring Marrakech and enjoying outdoor excursions.

Should you book attractions in advance?

Yes, especially for the Majorelle Garden, popular day trips and desert activities, which often sell out during school holidays and weekends.

Is Marrakech suitable for a family trip?

Yes. Gardens, horse-drawn carriage rides, Atlas Mountain excursions, activities in the Palmeraie and traditional shows provide plenty of family-friendly experiences.
