🏨 Where to Stay in Miami?

From the iconic beaches of Miami Beach to the modern skyscrapers of Brickell and the quieter coastal areas, each neighborhood offers a different way to experience Miami.

🌴 South Beach

Miami's most iconic neighborhood. Perfect for a first visit thanks to its beaches, Art Deco hotels, Ocean Drive and lively atmosphere day and night.

🏙️ Brickell

Miami's modern financial district. With its rooftops, stylish restaurants, luxury hotels and impressive skyline, Brickell is ideal for travelers seeking an upscale urban experience.

🌊 Downtown Miami

Close to Bayside Marketplace, the cruise port and the Metromover, Downtown is an excellent base for exploring the city while enjoying spectacular views over Biscayne Bay.

🏝️ Key Biscayne

Just minutes from Downtown, Key Biscayne offers beautiful beaches, preserved natural areas and a much quieter atmosphere than Miami Beach.

☀️ Sunny Isles Beach

Located north of Miami Beach, Sunny Isles is known for its long sandy beaches, oceanfront resorts and more relaxed atmosphere. A great choice for travelers seeking comfort and relaxation.

🎬 Miami in Pop Culture

From cult TV shows and legendary movies to video games and iconic nightlife, Miami has built a unique image in global popular culture. A different way to discover the city through the places that inspired generations.

The City That Inspired Hollywood

From Miami Vice and Scarface to Dexter, Bad Boys and GTA Vice City, Miami has become one of the most recognizable cities in popular culture.

🚤 Miami Vice

The iconic 1980s series popularized sports cars, pastel suits, speedboats and the glamorous atmosphere of Miami Beach. It played a major role in shaping the city's modern image.

💰 Scarface

The cult film starring Al Pacino helped build Miami's legendary reputation. Several iconic locations across the city still evoke the world of Tony Montana.

🔪 Dexter

The famous series featuring Miami's most notorious forensic expert used numerous city-inspired settings, further strengthening Miami's place in popular culture.

🚔 Bad Boys

The explosive adventures of Mike Lowrey and Marcus Burnett delivered some of the most memorable action scenes ever filmed in Miami, from beaches to highways and skyscrapers.

🎮 GTA Vice City

Directly inspired by Miami, Vice City became one of the most iconic video games of all time thanks to its neon lights, palm trees and unforgettable retro atmosphere.

💃 Mango's Tropical Cafe

With dancers, Latin music, cocktails and a festive atmosphere, Mango's Tropical Cafe has become one of Ocean Drive's most iconic venues. An experience that perfectly captures Miami's energy after sunset.
